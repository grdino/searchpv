# SearchPV Development Profile Writing Style Guide

## Purpose

SearchPV development profiles should help buyers, sellers, and investors understand a building or subdivision clearly. The tone should be professional, data-aware, and useful — not promotional.

## Core Voice

SearchPV should sound:

- Objective
- Calm
- Practical
- Locally informed
- Helpful to buyers and sellers
- Data-supported when possible

Avoid sounding like:

- Developer marketing
- MLS remarks
- Hype copy
- A sales brochure
- Generic AI-written travel content

## Editorial Rules

### 1. Be descriptive, not promotional

Avoid:
> EVA is an amazing luxury condominium with incredible amenities.

Prefer:
> EVA is a contemporary condominium development in Emiliano Zapata, known for its central location, modern construction, and walkability to the Romantic Zone.

### 2. Do not overstate facts

Use careful language when something may vary by unit or is not fully confirmed.

Good phrases:
- "typically"
- "generally"
- "may include"
- "depending on the unit"
- "based on current MLS data"
- "according to available information"

Avoid:
- "always"
- "guaranteed"
- "the best"
- "world-class"
- "unmatched"
- "perfect investment"

### 3. Separate stable facts from live market commentary

Static profile content should include things that rarely change:

- Overview
- Building facts
- Amenities
- Location
- Lifestyle
- Buyer notes
- Investor notes

Live SearchPV Insights should be generated from current market data:

- Active listings
- Pending listings
- Closed sales
- Months of inventory
- Median sold price
- Average sold price per ft²
- Average DOM

### 4. Explain why details matter

Instead of only saying:
> The building has an elevator.

Prefer:
> Elevator access is especially important for buyers comparing walkable Romantic Zone buildings, where some older properties may have limited accessibility.

### 5. Keep sections concise

Most sections should be 1–2 short paragraphs. Development pages should feel informative, not overwhelming.

### 6. Be honest about uncertainty

If something is incomplete, say so clearly or leave it out. Do not invent amenities, policies, developers, unit counts, or completion dates.

### 7. Use SearchPV Insights as the data-driven voice

SearchPV Insights should interpret the current numbers without becoming a prediction.

Good:
> With approximately 4.2 months of inventory, supply appears relatively balanced against recent demand.

Avoid:
> Prices will continue rising.

## Section Guidance

### Overview

Describe what the development is, where it is, and why buyers tend to consider it.

### Building Facts

Use factual details only: units, floors, construction status, year built, developer, building type.

### Amenities

List amenities only when reasonably confirmed. If amenities vary or are uncertain, use careful language.

### Location

Describe the neighborhood context, walkability, beach access, nearby services, and lifestyle convenience.

### Lifestyle

Explain who the development may appeal to: full-time residents, seasonal owners, vacation buyers, investors, golfers, marina users, etc.

### Buyer Notes

Practical considerations: parking, elevator access, HOA, noise, rental rules, view differences, age of building, maintenance, inventory scarcity.

### Investor Notes

Rental demand, walkability, building recognition, amenities, likely tenant profile, and policy considerations. Do not promise returns.

### SearchPV Insights

Generated dynamically from live MLS data whenever possible. Manual override should be used only when a human-written market interpretation is clearly better than the generated one.

## Standard Language for Dynamic Insights

Use phrases like:

- "Based on the latest SearchPV MLS snapshot..."
- "Current supply appears..."
- "Recent sales show..."
- "There is not enough recent closed-sales activity to calculate..."
- "Compared with the recent sales pace..."

Avoid predictions and unsupported claims.

## Final Test

Before publishing a profile, ask:

1. Is every factual claim supportable?
2. Does it sound like SearchPV rather than a sales brochure?
3. Would this help a serious buyer make a better decision?
4. Is any market commentary tied to live data rather than stale text?
